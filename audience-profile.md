# Audience Profile

Who reads most of your output: [e.g., High-net-worth clients, small business owners, tech executives with RSUs, retired physicians, young professionals]

What they care about: [What keeps them up at night financially? What are their goals? What's personal about their relationship with money? e.g., "Growing and protecting the business they built from scratch. Client retention, practice efficiency, staying competitive without a big brand behind them."]

How much they already know about your domain: [Are they financially sophisticated? Do they need hand-holding on basics, or can you skip to strategy? e.g., "High, and often broad. Don't talk down to them, but don't assume they've gone deep on every niche topic."]

How they prefer to receive information: [Bullets or paragraphs? Summary or detail? Formal or casual? e.g., "Direct and practical. They'd rather read something that sounds like a smart friend at a conference than a white paper from a compliance department."]

What turns them off or makes them disengage: [What makes them stop reading? e.g., "Cookie-cutter advice. Anything that feels corporate, generic, or mass-produced. They can smell a vendor pitch from a mile away."]

The communication culture: [How do your clients talk? What's the vibe? e.g., "Smart-casual with substance. They respect authenticity over polish, but they still expect you to know your stuff cold."]
